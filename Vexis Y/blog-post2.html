The Virtual Factory: Optimizing Production with Digital Twins

Emily White

November 19, 2023

Imagine being able to build, test, and optimize an entire production line in a virtual world before investing in any physical hardware. This is the power of digital twin technology, a concept that is revolutionizing how we approach robotic manufacturing. A digital twin is a dynamic, virtual replica of a physical asset, process, or system, updated in real-time with data from its physical counterpart.

Design and Commissioning at Light Speed

With a digital twin, engineers can design and simulate robotic workcells in a virtual environment. They can test different layouts, program robot paths, and validate cycle times without disrupting the existing factory floor. This drastically reduces the time and cost of commissioning new production lines, allowing for faster innovation and product launches.

A Playground for 'What-If' Scenarios

Once a production line is operational, its digital twin becomes an invaluable tool for continuous improvement. Plant managers can run 'what-if' scenarios to see how changes might impact performance. What if we increase the robot's speed? What if we introduce a new product variant? The digital twin provides the answers, enabling data-driven decisions that boost efficiency and throughput without risk.

At Vexis, we leverage digital twin technology to de-risk our clients' investments and accelerate their journey to automation excellence. We build the factory of the future, first in the virtual world, then in the real one.
