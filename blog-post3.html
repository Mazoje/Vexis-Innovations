AI as a Creative Partner: The Power of Generative Design

Micheal Brown

November 26, 2023

For centuries, human engineers have relied on their experience and intuition to design parts. But what if we could partner with an AI that could explore thousands of design possibilities in the time it takes to drink a coffee? This is the reality of generative design, an iterative design process that uses AI to generate optimal designs based on a set of constraints.

How it Works

An engineer provides the generative design software with a set of goals and constraints. These include parameters like the material to be used, the manufacturing method (e.g., 3D printing, CNC milling), required strength, and weight limits. The AI then explores a vast design space, creating hundreds or even thousands of design options that meet the criteria. The results are often organic, lattice-like structures that are incredibly strong yet lightweightâ€”designs a human engineer might never conceive.

Applications in Robotics

This technology is a game-changer for robotics manufacturing. We can use generative design to create lighter, stronger robot end-effectors (the 'hands' of the robot), more efficient cooling channels for machinery, and optimized brackets and chassis. The result is robots that are faster, more energy-efficient, and capable of handling more complex tasks. When combined with additive manufacturing (3D printing), these complex designs can be brought to life with unprecedented speed.

Vexis Innovations is harnessing the power of generative design to build the next generation of robotic hardware, creating machines that are not just intelligent, but optimally designed from the inside out.
